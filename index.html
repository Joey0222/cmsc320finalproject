Data Tidying¶

Now that we have obtained the data, and put it all into a table, we can begin tidying the data to make it fit our specific experiment.

We can begin by removing unwanted information from the dataframe. This includes:

  • Data from years that aren't 2013, the year our investigation is based on.
  • Statistics that aren't relevant to our investigation: this includes most of the columns except: Name, Year, Games, Wins, Losses, Runs allowed, Runs scored, Hits allowed, Hits, and a few others to keep track of the teams.

In order to do this, we will simply modify the sql query and recreate the dataframe. This allows us to rename the columns as we extract the information, making the entire process more streamlined.

Now that the table is tidy, I will elaborate on each of the columns for you to get a better understanding of what they represent.

Team : TeamID as represented in the database: ARI = Arizona Diamondbacks

Rank : Their ranking in their division (Each league has 3 divisions: West, East, and Central hence the repeats in rank)

League/Division : Their League (American League or National League) and division (West, East or Central)

Games/Wins/Losses : Games played, Games won, and Games lost

Runs_Scored/Runs_Allowed : Cumulative runs scored and allowed throughout the season (Run = Point in baseball)

ERA : Average earned runs allowed per game (Different from runs allowed as runs earned off of fielder errors are not counted. This makes ERA a primarily pitching statistic)

AtBats/Hits/Hits_Allowed : Team cumulative at bats and hits/hits allowed during the season

BPF/PPF : Three-year park factor for batters/pitchers, will be explained more in detail later

BA : Team batting average for the 2013 season

Data Adjustment and Standardization¶

After the initial analysis we have a general idea on where the Astros stand in 2013. While they are definitely one of the worst teams, we need to find a way to quantify definitively whether or not they were the worst.

The way we are going to do this is to measure their expected win percentage using the Sabrmetrics method.

The equation for expected win percentage is EXP(W%) = (RS)^2 / [(RS)^2 + (RA)^2] where RS = Runs scored, and RA = Runs Allowed

However, it is not as simple as plugging in the numbers. If we want to get a complete accurate assessment of the 2013 season, we will have to factor in ballpark favorability. This is where BPF and PPF come in.

BPF/PFF, or Batting Park Factor is a statistic that quantifies the contribution of a team's home stadium to their offensive production as well as pitching production.

More Information on BPF/PFF: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Batting_park_factor

In order to get an accurate representation of how many runs a scores/allowed, we can adjust using the Park Factor to get a more standardized playing field to analyze team's expected winning percentage.

To do this, we will create a new dataframe that contains only a few columns of key data:

  • Team Name (TEAM)

  • Runs Scored/Runs Allowed (RS, RA)

  • BPF and PFF (BPF, PFF)

  • Adjusted Runs Scored/Runs Allowed (ARS, ARA)

  • True Expected Winning Percentage (TEWP)

In [115]:
#Make a new dataframe
newData = pd.DataFrame()

#Set team, Runs scored, and runs allowed from tstats
newData['TEAM'] = tstats['Team']
newData['RS'] = tstats['Runs_Scored']
newData['RA'] = tstats['Runs_Allowed']

#turn the BPF and PFF into multipliers to modify the adjusted runs scored/allowed
newData['BPF'] = tstats['BPF']/100
newData['PPF'] = tstats['PPF']/100

#create the adjusted runs scored/allowed
newData['ARS'] = newData['RS'] * newData['BPF']
newData['ARA'] = newData['RA'] * newData['PPF']

#using the adjusted runs scored and allowed, we create the True Expected Win Percentage
newData['TEWP'] = ((newData['ARS']*newData['ARS'])/((newData['ARS']*newData['ARS'])+(newData['ARA']*newData['ARA'])))

#sort the dataframe by TEWP
newData.sort_values('TEWP', inplace=True)
#plot the data in a bar plot
ax = newData.plot.bar(x='TEAM', y='TEWP', color = "grey", figsize=(10,10))
#title the plot
ax.title.set_text('MLB 2013 True Expected Winning Percentage By Team')
#labeling for x and y axis
ax.set_xlabel('Team')
ax.set_ylabel('True Expected Winning Percentage')

#show the mean (0.5, or 50% winning percentage)
plt.axhline(y=newData['TEWP'].mean(), color='k', linestyle='--', linewidth=1) 
#set the limits to make the plot look nicer
plt.ylim((0.25, 0.7))
#show the plot
plt.show()

Now that we have the true expected win percentage, we can clearly see that the Houston Astros are at the bottom of the chart. This is not looking good for them. Now in order to get the definitive rankings of the teams in 2013, we will standardize the true winning percentage to see how bad the 2013 Houston Astros truly were.

In order to standardize the TEWP, we will have to first get the mean TEWP for 2013, as well as the standard deviation. We can then apply the standardization formula to create a new column in the newData dataframe to represent the standardized TEWP for all teams in 2013.

Conclusion¶

So were the 2013 Astros the worst team in the league?

During our investigation, we investigated the Houston Astros' performance in the 2013 season using a number of metrics. The first analysis we did was comparing their ERA and BA to the rest of the league. In addition we used BPF and PPF to adjust their runs allowed and scored to calculate their true expected winning percentage of the season. Using all this analysis, I have come to the conclusion that yes, the Houston Astros were the worst team in baseball in 2013, and it wasn't really close.

Judging off their standardized true expected winning percentage of -2, a fairly unbias datapoint given the adjustments made to runs scored/allowed using the PPF and BPF, as well as the fact that it is a standardized statistic, we can decisively conclude that the Houston Astros were the worst team in baseball in the MLB 2013 season. Not only did they have the worst record, worst run differential, worst team ERA and below average team BA, they also had the worst standardized true expected winning percentage cementing them as the worst team in the league by a fairly large margain.
